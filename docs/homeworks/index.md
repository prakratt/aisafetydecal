## Assignments

This course has two kinds of graded work: **weekly reflections** and a **final project**. Together with attendance, they make up your course grade.

### Weekly Reflections (30%)

Reflections are short written responses to each week's assigned readings, **submitted before each class**. You'll answer specific questions about the material and add your own insights — points of interest, connections to other ideas, and areas of agreement or disagreement.

- Expected total time per week (readings + reflection): **~90 minutes**
- **Two reflections may be missed** without penalty
- One reflection per week, for ten weeks

### Final Project (30%)

You'll complete a coding project focused on **replicating results from a technical AI/ML safety paper**, optionally extending the ideas for extra credit. The project is designed to take about **8 hours** and must be accompanied by a **conference / workshop-style research paper** (short LaTeX writeup).

- Groups: **1–3 students** (groups must be approved beforehand)
- Passing grade requires at least 70%
- Showcase in the final week

See the [Final Project](final-project.html) page for milestones, paper-template, and replication suggestions.

### Paper Presentation

Each student will give a short, in-class summary of one of the papers read during a discussion section. Sign-ups are first-come-first-serve.
