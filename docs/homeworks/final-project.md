## Final Project

Your final project is the capstone of this course. You will **replicate results from a technical AI/ML safety paper**, optionally extending the ideas in a creative direction for extra credit.

### Logistics

- **Time:** Designed for ~8 hours of work
- **Groups:** 1–3 students (groups must be **approved beforehand**)
- **Deliverables:**
  1. A reproducible code artifact (GitHub repo)
  2. A short **conference / workshop-style research paper** (4–8 pages, NeurIPS or ICML-style)
  3. A **showcase presentation** in the final week
- **Passing grade:** at least 70%

### Replication Targets

Any paper from the course [Reading List](/aisafety/docs/resources/) is fair game, but here are some that historically work well as student replications:

- **GCG** — Universal & Transferable Adversarial Attacks ([Zou et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2307.15043))
- **DPO** — Direct Preference Optimization ([Rafailov et al. 2023](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.18290))
- **Steering vectors** — [Activation Addition (Turner et al. 2023)](https://arxiv.org/abs/2308.10248)
- **Sparse Autoencoders** — small-scale SAE on a tiny LM, in the spirit of [Scaling Monosemanticity](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2024/scaling-monosemanticity/)
- **WMDP** — partial replication of the [WMDP unlearning benchmark](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.03218)
- **Specification gaming / reward hacking** — design a small toy environment in the style of [Krakovna et al.](https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/specification-gaming-the-flip-side-of-ai-ingenuity/)

### Paper Template

We recommend writing your paper in **LaTeX** using the NeurIPS template.

- [NeurIPS 2024 LaTeX style files](https://media.neurips.cc/Conferences/NeurIPS2024/Styles.zip)
- [Overleaf NeurIPS template](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/neurips-2024/tpsbbrdqcmsh)

### Academic Integrity

You **may not** use LLMs to write your code unless you have **explicit written permission** from the instructors. Permission is granted on a case-by-case basis for projects that go above and beyond the course material (e.g. original research). You **may** use LLMs to discuss concepts and receive conceptual guidance. Cite any non-trivial outside code.
